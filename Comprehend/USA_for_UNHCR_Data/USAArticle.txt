It was only after the dust from the air strike settled that Fareed al-Mhlol realised his home had taken a direct hit.The walls of his house in the town of Ma'arat al-Nu'man, in the northwestern Syrian province of Idlib, had crumbled in, injuring his entire family and killing his aunt.Rebel-held Idlib on Sunday faced its heaviest day of strikes since the latest government offensive began on April 30, according to the war monitor Syrian Observatory for Human Rights.Bombs have been raining down indiscriminately on residential areas, on schools and on hospitals for weeks, as President Bashar al-Assads forces attempt to retake the final opposition stronghold. I ran first towards my mother and my sisters. I took them out of the house where I thought they would be safer Mr Mhlol told the Telegraph by phone on Monday.
